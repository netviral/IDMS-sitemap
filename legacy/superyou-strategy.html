Step 01 — Website Goal

The one job
the site must do

Help a skeptical, health-conscious Indian consumer understand why fermented yeast protein is better for their gut — and buy their first pack in under 3 minutes.
The core tension

SuperYou sells an unfamiliar ingredient in a crowded category. The site must educate fast and sell confidently at the same time — without one undermining the other.

Business goals — from site signals
  • Convert first-time visitors on a novel product format
  • Build a subscription base via SuperYou Pro + Refer & Earn
  • Establish topical authority in fermented protein (20+ blog posts)
  • Grow community through merchandise (shakers, Pro program)
What the site is not doing yet
  • No quiz or guided "start here" flow for new visitors
  • Pro & affiliate program buried — not in the purchase funnel
  • Blog and commerce live in separate silos

Step 06 — Interaction

From browse-and-buy
to decide-and-stay.

Priority 1
Blog → Product Bridge

Inline product cards inside every blog post. Gut health article → protein powder CTA. The single highest-ROI interaction change available today. Blogs are currently a dead end.

Priority 2
"Find Your Protein" Quiz

3 questions: dietary preference → health goal → lifestyle. Output: a recommended product + blog post pair. Reduces decision fatigue for first-timers. Particularly powerful on mobile.

Priority 3
Ingredient Explainer

A scroll-driven animated breakdown of the fermentation process. Makes the unfamiliar feel premium. Not a blog post — a standalone experience page. Think Ritual vitamins or AG1.

Priority 4
Bundle Customiser

Build your stack — protein + creatine + shaker. Live price update. Surfaces the bundling logic that already exists in the catalogue but is invisible to visitors.

Priority 5
Subscription Toggle on PDP

One-time vs Subscribe & Save. Dramatically increases LTV for a consumable product. Especially powerful on powders and creatine where consistency is the use case.

Priority 6 · Aspirational
30-Day Gut Tracker

Logged-in experience tied to SuperYou Pro. Weekly wellbeing check-ins. Builds loyalty and generates testimonials organically. Turns the SuperYou Pro page from a promo into a product.

Step 07 — Wow Factor

Every competitor sells macros.
SuperYou sells how it feels.

The single biggest opportunity: own "Gut-First Protein" with an interactive, immersive ingredient story that no Indian protein brand is doing.
01
The Fermentation Story Page

Full-screen, scroll-animated page: grain → fermentation tank → protein extraction → your gut. Built like an editorial feature, not a FAQ. Gives visitors something to share and a claim no competitor can copy quickly.

02
First 30 Days Journey

Interactive post-purchase onboarding. Day 1, 7, 14, 30 content. The blog "New to Protein? Give Your Gut Time" already exists — this is the interactive version. Turns the most common drop-off point into a retention moment.

03
The Protein Zone as Brand Universe

The blog is called "The Protein Zone" — that's a brand asset. Give it its own visual identity, issue numbers, featured reads. Makes content feel curated, not just keyword-targeted. Press-worthy.

04
Taste-First Visual Language

Choco Peanut Butter. Strawberry Crème. Cold Coffee. These are craveable snack flavours, not clinical supplement names. The site should lead with taste photography the way a snack brand would. That dissonance between "it's a supplement" and "it looks like a snack" is the wow factor.
